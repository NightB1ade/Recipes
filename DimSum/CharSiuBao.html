--- title: Char Siu Bao basePortions: 10 ---

Source

thewoksoflife.com/steamed-bbq-pork-buns-char-siu-bao

Ingredients

  • g active dry yeast
  • ml warm water
  • g plain flour
  • g corn flour
  • g sugar
  • ml vegetable oil
  • tsp (g) baking powder
  • - tsp water (optional)
  • g char siu, finely chopped

Method

  1. In the bowl of an electric mixer fitted with a dough hook attachment, dissolve the yeast in the warm water
  2. Sift together the flour and corn flour, and add it to the yeast mixture along with the sugar and oil
  3. Turn on the mixer to the lowest setting and let it go until a smooth dough ball is formed
  4. Cover with a damp cloth and let it rest for 2 hours
  5. After your dough has rested for 2 hours, add the baking powder to the dough and turn the mixer on to the lowest setting. At this point, if the dough looks dry or you’re having trouble incorporating the baking powder, add 1- teaspoons water
  6. Gently knead the dough until it becomes smooth again
  7. Cover with a damp cloth and let it rest for another 15 minutes
  8. Meanwhile, cut baking paper into 2×2 inch squares
  9. Prepare your steamer by bringing the water to a boil
  10. Now we are ready to assemble the buns: Roll the dough into a long tube and divide it into equal pieces
  11. Press each piece of dough into a disc about 4.5 inches in diameter (it should be thicker in the centre and thinner around the edges)
  12. Add some filling and pleat the buns until they’re closed on top
  13. Place each bun on a baking paper square
  14. Once the water boils, place the buns in the steamer (2 at a time) and steam each batch for 12 minutes over high heat
